Legal and Tax Considerations

If you are a Resident Indian (RI), which means you did not complete 183 days of sailing, you may need to declare your SeaBoard account for tax purposes. To do so, you will need to transfer the money from your SeaBoard account to your home country bank account and declare it to relevant authorities.

If you are a Non-Resident-Indian (NRI), which means you have completed 183 days of sailing, you may not need to declare the SeaBoard account. There is no limit to how much you can hold in your account. However, you may need to do so if you become a RI.

If you are unsure of your residential status, please consult with a chartered accountant (CA) or a tax specialist.

References: Income Tax Act, 1961, Section 6

Account Setup

You will need to provide a copy of your Continuous Discharge Certificate (CDC) and passport to set up a SeaBoard account. Please ensure that the documents:

  1. Are clear; not cropped
  2. Contain the front and back pages
  3. Have not expired

Your SeaBoard account will take approximately 2 to 3 working days to be ready, as we will need to verify your documents for security purposes.

Once your SeaBoard account is ready, we will issue your physical card within 2 working days. We will also keep you updated on your card issuance status in the app.

Each SeaBoard account has a unique alpha-numeric wallet identifier (example: SB 1234 567 890) which you can find in the app.

You can share this wallet identifier with your employer, ship master or other SeaBoard app users to receive wages or money.

Your wallet remains active for 1 year after ship contract ends
